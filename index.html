Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most Chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Camel

  • Camels are known as ship of the desert.
  • Newborn camels don't have humps. They gradually grow as they get older.
  • Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.

Panda

  • Pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.
  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm.

Cicada

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  • The name "cicada" means "tree cricket" in Latin. This refers to the loud, cricket-like noise that cicadas produce during the summer days.
  • Cicadas spend most of their life under ground.

Teddy bear

  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
